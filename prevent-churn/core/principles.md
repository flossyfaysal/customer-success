# Core Principles

## Guiding Principles

1. **Acknowledge First, Solve Second**: Always validate the customer's frustration before jumping to solutions
2. **Offer Concrete Help**: Provide specific, actionable solutions (not vague promises)
3. **Partnership Language**: Use "we," "us," and "together" to emphasize collaboration
4. **Safety Net**: Always offer the refund as a backup if solutions don't work
5. **Personal Touch**: Write as a human who genuinely cares, not as corporate script

---

## Input Requirements

When using this skill, you'll need:
- **Customer's complaint/refund request**: The exact issue they're facing
- **Product/service context**: What they were trying to use (backup, migration, plugin, etc.)
- **Frustration level** (optional): Low, Medium, High — helps calibrate response tone
- **Previous attempts** (optional): What the customer has already tried
- **Customer history** (optional): Tenure, number of purchases, activation history

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't minimize their frustration: Avoid "it's just a simple issue" or "this rarely happens"
- Don't blame the customer: Never "you should have..." or "did you try..."
- Don't be vague: Avoid "we'll look into it" without specific actions
- Don't oversell: Don't promise things you can't deliver
- Don't be defensive: Even if they're wrong, stay humble and helpful
- Don't use corporate templates: No "we value your feedback" boilerplate
- Don't ignore the refund request: Always acknowledge it and provide the safety net
- Don't make them jump through hoops: Make saying "yes" to help easy

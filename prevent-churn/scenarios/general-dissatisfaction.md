# Scenario: General Dissatisfaction / Feature Gaps

## Common Issues
- Product doesn't meet the customer's specific needs
- Missing a feature they expected to be there
- Poor or unclear documentation
- Workflow doesn't match what was advertised

## Key Solutions to Offer
- Detailed walkthrough of features that might solve their underlying need
- Custom solution or workaround for their use case
- Feature request prioritization with the development team
- Access to beta features if applicable
- Partial refund while keeping the account active

## Example Opening
"I'm sorry our product hasn't lived up to your expectations. That's disappointing for both of us — we want you to feel like this was a great decision, and clearly we've missed the mark somewhere. I'd love to understand what you need and see if we can bridge that gap."

## Approach Pattern
1. **Understand the unmet need** — ask what they expected, not what went wrong
2. **Bridge the gap** — show features or workflows they may not have discovered
3. **Build a custom path** — if the feature exists in a different form, configure it for them
4. **Feature request** — if the gap is real, log it and give them a timeline if possible
5. **Partial retention offer** — partial refund while keeping access, so they can benefit from future updates
6. **Safety net** — full refund if none of this works

## Why This Approach Works
- Focusing on the unmet need (not the complaint) opens a collaborative conversation
- Showing hidden features makes the customer feel like they gained something, not just justified their frustration
- A partial refund offer is a strong middle-ground that retains the relationship without full loss

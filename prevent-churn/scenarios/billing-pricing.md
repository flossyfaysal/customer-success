# Scenario: Billing/Pricing Concerns

## Common Issues
- Unexpected renewal charges
- Pricing not matching what the customer remembered
- Unclear renewal terms at time of purchase
- Auto-renewal not communicated clearly

## Key Solutions to Offer
- Clear, line-by-line breakdown of the charge
- Pricing adjustment, credit, or partial refund
- Flexible payment arrangement
- Grandfather clause for future renewals

## Example Opening
"I'm truly sorry for the confusion around your billing. Unexpected charges are never a good surprise, and I completely understand why you'd want to address this. Let me see how I can make this right."

## Approach Pattern
1. **Clarify without blame** — explain what happened clearly and factually
2. **Acknowledge the surprise** — even if the charge was correct, the experience of being surprised is valid
3. **Offer a resolution** — credit, adjustment, or refund depending on circumstances
4. **Improve clarity going forward** — offer to set reminders or clarify renewal terms
5. **Safety net** — full refund if they prefer not to continue

## Why This Approach Works
- Never making the customer feel stupid for missing a renewal notice builds goodwill
- Offering a credit or adjustment shows flexibility even when the charge was technically correct
- Improving clarity going forward shows you care about the ongoing relationship, not just this transaction

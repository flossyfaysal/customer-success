# Scenario: Renewal Refund Request

## Key Policy
Renewals are non-refundable per Duplicator's terms of service:
https://duplicator.com/terms/#refunds

Do NOT offer a full refund for renewal orders. Instead, follow the partial discount approach below.

---

## Required Inputs — Ask Before Replying

Before writing the reply, you MUST collect the following from the agent:

1. **Is this a renewal order or a new purchase?**
   - If new purchase (within 14 days): standard refund applies — exit this scenario and follow the standard refund flow
   - If renewal: continue with this scenario

2. **How many sites are registered on the customer's license?**
   - This helps frame the value of keeping the license active

3. **Was the 30-day reminder email sent to the customer?**
   - Ask the agent to confirm and provide the email if available

4. **Was the 1-day reminder email sent to the customer?**
   - Ask the agent to confirm and provide the email if available

---

## Discount to Offer

Default offer: **50% partial refund** on the renewal

Use 30% or 60% only when instructed by a manager or when the situation clearly warrants it.

---

## Reply Template

Use the sections below. Include or skip conditional blocks based on the situation.

---

Hi [Customer Name],

I apologize for any confusion about renewals!

[[INCLUDE IF CUSTOMER MENTIONS THEY DIDN'T KNOW ABOUT AUTO-RENEWAL]]
To help clarify: when you purchased your Duplicator subscription, you agreed to automatic renewal. Here's a screenshot showing how this is displayed on the checkout page: https://a.supportally.com/i/umjYE3

I'm sorry if this felt unclear in any way. The option to cancel auto-renewal is always available directly in your account, and we want to make sure that's easy to find.

To give advance notice, we send two reminder emails - one a month before renewal and one a day before - along with the renewal price. If you didn't receive these, they may have been filtered into spam. [[INSERT REMINDER EMAIL DETAILS HERE IF AVAILABLE]]

We're not looking to mislead anyone. If there's anything that could have made this clearer, we'd genuinely appreciate your feedback.
[[END CONDITIONAL BLOCK]]

[[INCLUDE IF CUSTOMER IS REQUESTING A RENEWAL REFUND AND NO DISCOUNT HAS BEEN OFFERED YET]]
As outlined in our terms of service, renewals are non-refundable. However, we don't want to leave you feeling stuck, so we're happy to make an exception and offer a 50% discount on your recent renewal through a partial refund.

That way, you'd be able to continue using Duplicator Pro for another year at a significant discount.

Please note: this is a one-time offer, and all future renewals will bill at full price.

If you'd like to take us up on this, just let me know and I'll get that partial refund processed for you right away.
[[END CONDITIONAL BLOCK]]

Could you please let me know if you have any questions?

Thanks!

---

## Why This Approach Works

- Acknowledging the surprise builds goodwill without admitting fault
- Sharing the checkout screenshot and reminder email history shifts the conversation to facts without being confrontational
- The partial refund offer gives the customer a win while keeping them on the license
- Framing it as a one-time exception creates urgency without pressure
- Never promising a full refund keeps the policy intact while still feeling generous

---

## What NOT to Do

- Do NOT offer a full refund for a renewal order without manager approval
- Do NOT skip asking whether reminder emails were sent - this is important context
- Do NOT be defensive or make the customer feel blamed for missing the renewal notice
- Do NOT offer 60% without manager approval

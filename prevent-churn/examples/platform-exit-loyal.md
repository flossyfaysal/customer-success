# Example: Platform Exit — Long-Term Loyal Customer

**Customer Context**: "The license just literally renews today. I have been a long-time customer and am requesting a refund as we have no use for this plug-in any longer as we are not using WordPress at all. I hope you consider my request."

**Account history**: 6 years as a customer, license activated across multiple sites

**Scenario**: [Platform Exit](../scenarios/platform-exit.md)
**Frustration Level**: None / Appreciation Churn

---

## Response

Hi there,

Thank you so much for reaching out, and thank you even more for being with us for 6 years. That means a great deal to our team, and we genuinely appreciate the trust you placed in Duplicator over that time.

I can see your license was activated across several sites — which tells me you put it to real use and had hands-on experience with the product. Knowing that makes this bittersweet, but we completely understand that moving away from WordPress is the right decision for your business, and we respect it fully.

I've gone ahead and processed your refund in full. You'll see it reflected within a few business days.

Before you go, I'd love to ask a small personal favor. Since you've had genuine, long-term experience with Duplicator across multiple projects, would you be willing to leave us a quick review on Trustpilot? It would mean a lot to our small team and helps other WordPress users make an informed decision. No pressure at all — just a kind ask from someone who's worked with us for a long time.

You're always welcome back if you ever return to WordPress. We'll be here.

Wishing you all the best in your new direction.

Best regards,
[Your Name]

---

## What Made This Response Work

- **Honored the refund immediately**: No resistance — the reason was genuine and legitimate
- **Referenced specific history**: "6 years" and "activated across multiple sites" made the appreciation feel real, not scripted
- **Trustpilot ask framed as a personal favor**: Not a business request — a human ask from someone who cared about their experience
- **Open door close**: Low cost, high goodwill — keeps the relationship alive for a potential return

# Loyalty Appreciation Phrases

Use these with long-term customers, especially in graceful exit (appreciation churn) scenarios. Reference their actual history — years, activations, purchases — to make it feel genuine rather than scripted.

## Acknowledging Tenure
- "I can see you've been with us for [X] years, and we truly appreciate your loyalty"
- "You've been a long-time customer, and that means a great deal to our team"
- "Being with us for [X] years is something we don't take for granted — thank you"

## Acknowledging Positive Usage
- "Your license has been activated across multiple sites, which tells us you got real, hands-on value from Duplicator"
- "I can see the impact you've made using our plugin across your projects"
- "The fact that you've used us across [X] sites shows the trust you placed in our product — we're genuinely proud of that"

## Requesting a Review (as a favor)
- "Before you go, I'd love to ask a small favor — would you be willing to leave us a quick review on Trustpilot?"
- "Since you've had genuine experience with the product, a short review from you would mean the world to our small team"
- "If you have a moment, a Trustpilot review from a long-time customer like yourself helps other WordPress users make informed decisions"

## Open Door Close
- "You're always welcome back if you ever return to WordPress — we'll be here"
- "If WordPress ever comes back into the picture, don't hesitate to reach out"
- "We'd love to have you back whenever the time is right"
- "Wishing you all the best in your new direction — and the door is always open"

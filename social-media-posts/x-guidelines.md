# Awesome Motive X (Twitter) Guidelines

## Overview

X is a fast-moving, text-first platform. Posts need to be sharp, direct, and immediate. The audience skims - you have one sentence to earn the next read. Write like you're talking to a developer, WordPress user, or small business owner who already knows the space.

Character limit: 280 characters per tweet. Use a thread (2-5 tweets) when the idea needs more room.

---

## X content templates by pillar

### 1. Customer wins

**Theme:** Lead with the outcome. Short, specific, credible.

**Single tweet template:**
We heard "[short paraphrased quote]" from a user this week.
That's why [one-line description of what was built/fixed] exists.
Worth it.

**Thread template (when more context is needed):**
Tweet 1: Hook - the problem or the outcome
Tweet 2: What we built or changed (user-facing only)
Tweet 3: The reaction or result
Tweet 4 (optional): What this means for users going forward

**Tips:**

- Lead with the most interesting or surprising line - don't bury it
- No screenshots, no customer names
- "A user told us..." is fine; "Our customer John at Acme..." is not

### 2. Team collaboration

**Theme:** Make collaboration visible without exposing internals. Short and human.

**Single tweet template:**
[The problem] + [the teams involved] + [the outcome]. That's it.
Sometimes the best solutions come from people working across different parts of the business.

**Thread template:**
Tweet 1: The challenge (1 sentence)
Tweet 2: Who came together and how it felt
Tweet 3: What shipped and why it matters to users

**Tips:**

- Brand names are fine (OptinMonster, Duplicator) - internal tools are not
- One clear takeaway per thread - don't stretch a simple story

### 3. Innovation and speed

**Theme:** Fast, concrete, outcome-focused. X rewards specificity.

**Single tweet template:**
Spotted a problem Monday. Shipped a fix by Friday.
[One line on what changed for users.]
This is how we work.

**Thread template:**
Tweet 1: The timeline (idea to ship)
Tweet 2: What the user problem was
Tweet 3: What we changed (user-facing)
Tweet 4: Early feedback or result

**Tips:**

- Numbers and timeframes make it credible ("4 days", "within a week")
- Don't mention the process or tooling - just the outcome
- Keep threads tight - 3-4 tweets is the sweet spot

## Positioning guide

Same core rules as LinkedIn and Facebook. Outcomes over mechanics. User impact over internal process.

### How to talk about AI

**Do this:**

- "We used AI to cut the time it takes users to [task] in half."
- "Less friction. Faster start. That's what the new AI feature does."

**Don't do this:**

- Naming models, APIs, or training approaches
- Previewing features not yet live

### How to talk about speed

**Do this:**

- "Idea on Monday. Live on Friday. That's the goal."
- "We move fast because our users can't afford to wait."

**Don't do this:**

- Describing internal frameworks, pipelines, or tools
- Implying you skip quality steps

## Governance and guardrails

### Allowed

- Anonymized customer outcomes
- Team collaboration stories (no internal detail)
- Live product features and improvements
- Short, punchy takes on the WordPress/small business space
- Threads for more complex stories

### Not allowed

- Internal tool names or systems
- Screenshots of internal docs or conversations
- Customer names or account details
- Unreleased features or roadmap hints
- Metrics not already public

---

## Format rules

- Single tweet: under 280 characters
- Thread: 2-5 tweets, each self-contained enough to make sense alone
- Hashtags: 2-3 max, integrated naturally into the text or at the very end - not stacked
- No markdown formatting - plain text only
- No emojis unless they genuinely fit (rare on X for professional content)
- Hook on the first line - assume the rest gets cut off

---

## Hashtag reference

Keep hashtags minimal and relevant. Examples:

- #WordPress
- #WPDev
- #SmallBusiness
- #BuildInPublic
- #ProductDev
- #CustomerSuccess

Avoid overloading. 2 hashtags is usually better than 5.

---

## Posting cadence

- 1 post per week minimum (Wednesdays)
- Threads only when the idea genuinely needs more than 280 characters
- Ask before posting: "Is this interesting enough that someone would stop scrolling? Is the first line strong enough on its own?"

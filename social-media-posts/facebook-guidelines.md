# Awesome Motive Facebook Guidelines

## Overview

Facebook posts for Awesome Motive should feel warmer and more conversational than LinkedIn. You're talking to an audience that includes existing customers, small business owners, and WordPress users - people who want to connect with the humans behind the products. Keep it relatable, grounded, and community-oriented.

Optimal length: 40-80 words per post. Longer is okay for storytelling, but cut anything that doesn't add value.

---

## Facebook content templates by pillar

### 1. Customer wins

**Theme:** Celebrate real user outcomes in a warm, community-friendly tone. Make followers feel like they're part of the win.

**Template:**
Someone told us this week: "[paraphrased customer feedback without identifying details]"
That's exactly why we built [feature/product].
If you've ever struggled with [common problem], you're not alone - and we've been working hard to fix it.
[Optional: soft CTA like "Let us know in the comments if you've run into this too."]

**Tips:**

- Write like you're talking to a friend, not writing a press release
- Paraphrase customer feedback - never include names, screenshots, or identifying details
- A soft question at the end boosts engagement naturally
- Emojis are okay here - use 1-2 max, only if they fit the tone

### 2. Team collaboration

**Theme:** Give followers a behind-the-scenes feel (without exposing internals). Show the human side of building software.

**Template:**
Building something good takes more than one person.
This week, [brief description of challenge] - and it took [functions/brands] working together to get it right.
When different perspectives come together around the same goal, the result is always better for users.
Proud of this team.

**Tips:**

- Keep the internal details vague - focus on feeling and outcome
- You can mention brands (OptinMonster, Duplicator, etc.) but not internal tools
- Warm, genuine tone - not a corporate announcement

### 3. Innovation and speed

**Theme:** Show how quickly Awesome Motive turns user needs into real solutions. Make followers feel like they're with a team that moves and cares.

**Template:**
We spotted a problem. We fixed it. Fast.
[One sentence describing the user-facing issue]
Within [timeframe], we shipped a solution - and the feedback since has been great.
This is what it looks like when a team stays focused on what users actually need.

**Tips:**

- Keep it grounded - don't over-hype
- Don't mention tools, processes, or anything not yet public
- A short, punchy structure works well on Facebook

## Positioning guide

Same core rules as LinkedIn - focus on outcomes, not mechanics. Talk about what users experience, not what happens under the hood.

### How to talk about AI

**Do this:**

- "We added an AI feature that helps users get started faster - no blank page anymore."
- "Our goal is to make this easier for everyone, and AI is helping us do that."

**Don't do this:**

- Naming the model, framework, or how the AI was trained
- Previewing AI features not yet launched

### How to talk about product work

**Do this:**

- "A small change we made last month is already saving users time every day."
- "We updated [feature] based on what users kept telling us - here's what's different."

**Don't do this:**

- Referencing internal metrics, experiments, or systems
- Teasing anything not yet live

---

## Governance and guardrails

### Allowed

- Customer outcomes and wins (anonymized)
- Team collaboration stories (no internal tools named)
- Product reflections on launched features
- Community questions and soft CTAs
- Emojis (1-2, used naturally)

### Not allowed

- Screenshots of internal tools, docs, or conversations
- Customer names or account details
- Unlaunched features or roadmap speculation
- Internal tool names or processes
- Metrics not already shared publicly

---

## Format rules

- Length: 40-80 words ideal; up to 150 for storytelling posts
- Paragraphs: 1-3 short paragraphs, max 2 sentences each
- Hashtags: 2-5, placed at the very end of the post
- Emojis: Optional, 1-2 max, only if they fit naturally
- No bold or markdown formatting - plain text only
- End with a question or soft CTA when it feels natural

---

## Hashtag reference

Use a mix of brand and topic hashtags. Examples:

- #AwesomeMotive
- #WordPress
- #SmallBusiness
- #WordPressTips
- #CustomerSuccess
- #ProductUpdate
- #TeamWork

---

## Posting cadence

- 1-2 posts per week, same days as LinkedIn where possible
- Wednesday is the primary posting day
- Ask before posting: "Does this feel human? Would a real customer find this interesting or relatable?"

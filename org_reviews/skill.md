# Skill: WP.org Review Reply

Generate a personalized, on-brand reply to a Duplicator Lite review on WordPress.org. Replies are **public** - every word is visible to thousands of potential users. Keep them casual, warm, short, and human. Never sound like a template.

---

## When to Use

- Replying to a WordPress.org review for Duplicator (free/Lite version)
- Any star rating (1 through 5)

---

## Required Inputs

1. **Star rating** (1, 2, 3, 4, or 5)
2. **Review text** (paste the full review)
3. **Reviewer username or name** (if visible — use it to personalize)

---

## Tone & Style Rules

1. **Casual and warm** - write like a friendly human, not a support bot
2. **Always vary the wording** - never use the same opener, structure, or closing twice. Rotate phrasing, sentence order, and energy
3. **Reference the review specifically** - quote or paraphrase what they said. Generic replies are worse than no reply
4. **Short** - 2-4 sentences max for positive reviews. Public replies should be scannable in seconds
5. **Emojis are encouraged** - use :) sparingly to keep it friendly and natural
6. **Greeting: "Hey @[username],"** - casual, no "Dear"
7. **Closing: vary it** - rotate between "Enjoy! :)", "Have a good one! :)", "We hope you continue to have a 5-star experience :)", or similar casual sign-offs. Never use "Best regards"
8. **Never ask for a rating change** - not directly, not hinted at. Ever.
9. **Never promote Duplicator Pro or any paid product** - forum replies are not a sales channel
10. **Never be defensive** - even on unfair reviews. Defensiveness damages the brand publicly
11. **For 1-3 star reviews**, always include the support link: https://wordpress.org/support/plugin/duplicator/

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't copy-paste any template verbatim** - always adapt to the specific review
- **Don't use "Best regards"** - too formal for this channel
- **Don't repeat the same opener or closing** across replies
- **Don't ask for a rating change** - directly or implicitly
- **Don't dismiss a complaint** with "this is unusual" or "we haven't seen this before"
- **Don't be verbose** - if it takes more than 4 sentences, trim it
- **Don't sound like a bot** - avoid corporate phrases like "We have received your feedback"
- **Don't ignore specifics** - a generic reply to a detailed review is worse than no reply
- **Don't promote Duplicator Pro or any paid product** - forum replies are not a sales channel
